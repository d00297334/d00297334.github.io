An unweighted GPA means that your GPA is measured on a scale from 0 to 4.0 regardless of how difficult your classes are. This means an A in an AP or IB course and an A in a low-level course both translate to 4.0s. You can use this GPA converter to figure out your unweighted GPA below.

Semester GPA is calculated by multiplying the number of credit hours assigned to a course by the value of the grade earned in that semester.

Cumulative Grade Point Average (GPA) refers to the overall GPA, which includes dividing the number of quality points earned in all courses attempted by the total degree-credit hours in all attempted courses.
